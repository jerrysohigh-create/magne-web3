Governance Overview

MAGNE.AI implements a decentralized governance model where $MHA token holders have the power to shape the future of the protocol through on-chain voting and proposals.

Governance Model

MAGNE uses a hybrid governance approach combining on-chain voting with off-chain discussion forums. This ensures both efficiency and community participation in decision-making.

Participation Requirements

  • Proposals: Any $MHA holder can submit a governance proposal
  • Voting Power: Determined by the amount of $MHA staked
  • Quorum: Minimum voting power required for a proposal to pass
  • Timelock: Approved changes are implemented after a delay period

Governance Categories

Protocol Upgrades

Changes to the core protocol including consensus mechanisms, block parameters, and network economics.

Treasury Management

Allocation of ecosystem funds for grants, partnerships, and development initiatives.

Parameter Adjustments

Fine-tuning of network parameters such as gas costs, staking requirements, and reward distributions.

Ecosystem Proposals

Suggestions for ecosystem growth, tooling improvements, and community initiatives.

Validator Governance

Validator nodes play a crucial role in network governance:

  • Selection: Based on proportion of $MHA tokens staked
  • Requirements: Meet staking and hardware requirements
  • Responsibilities: Validate transactions and participate in consensus
  • Rewards: Earn staking rewards proportional to stake
Note: The governance system is designed to be progressive, starting with core team guidance and transitioning to full community governance over time.
